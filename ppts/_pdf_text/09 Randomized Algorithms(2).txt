               Median and Selection
   • Median and Selection. Given n elements from a totally ordered universe,
     find the median element or in general the k-th smallest element.
             Ø minimum or maximum (k = 1 or k = n): O(n) compares
             Ø k-th smallest: (k = ⌊(n + 1) / 2⌋ for median)
               üO(n log n) compares by sorting
               üO(n log k) compares with a binary heap
   • Applications. Order statistics, find the “top k”, bottleneck paths, etc.

   • Q. Can we do it with O(n) compares?
   • A. Yes! Selection is easier than sorting.

               Global Minimum Cut
   • Global Minimum Cut. Given a connected, undirected graph G = (V, E),
     find a cut (A, B) of minimum cardinality.

   • Applications. Partitioning items in a database, identify clusters of related
     documents, network reliability, circuit design, TSP solvers, etc.

   • Network flow solution:
             Ø Replace every edge (u, v) with two antiparallel edges (u, v) and (v, u).
             Ø Pick any vertex s ∈ V: for every other node v ∈ V, compute min s-v cut.

   • False intuition. Global min-cut is harder than min s-t cut.

   • Centralized controller. Assign jobs in round-robin manner. Each

     How likely is it that some processor is assigned “too many” jobs?
